## Pending Decision Records (Compliance / AuditLog boundary) — RESOLVED by ADR-016

A Boundary Collision Gate (G1.5) run for a proposed `SPEC-COMPLIANCE-001` (target: `docs/03-modules/compliance/MODULE_SPEC.md`, currently only `.gitkeep`) found that the most direct CRR-derived owned-state claim for Compliance — the immutable `AuditLog` model (CRR §30 "every important action is logged... immutable"; `backend/prisma/schema.prisma:507-509` carries a `// COMPLIANCE MODULE //` section comment directly above the `AuditLog` model definition, the strongest direct code evidence in the repository that Compliance's real-world scope was meant to include this table) — was **not a clean, unclaimed state domain**. `state-audit-log` was recorded as a shared-write collision with `authoritative_writer: UNKNOWN` (`.claude/knowledge/STATE_OWNERSHIP_INDEX.yaml:27`), written via `BaseService.logAudit` from at least eight independent modules (`backend/src/modules/{attendance,quality,crm,users,auth,work-requests}/service.ts`, confirmed by direct grep), and flagged as an existing, unresolved human-authority item (`SYNC-005`; `SIR-GLOB-006`, first recorded 2026-07-04). Authoring `SPEC-COMPLIANCE-001` to claim ownership (read or write) of `AuditLog` would have required resolving — or silently assuming an answer to — that same pending human ownership decision. **`ADR-016` (Accepted, 2026-07-14) now settles this**: `backend-auth` is the authoritative writer of `state-audit-log`/`AuditLog`; `backend-compliance` is a read-only consumer, never a writer or lifecycle owner; Compliance's positive capability scope is settled as governance, verification, reporting, policy evaluation, evidence generation, and regulatory workflows. `SPEC-COMPLIANCE-001` may now be authored against this settled boundary. Compliance's other two candidate capabilities — GDPR subject-rights request orchestration (CRR §26) and governance/reporting consumption of Retention's deletion-audit surface (already disclaimed as Compliance's in `docs/03-modules/retention/MODULE_SPEC.md` Out of Scope, and referenced one-sidedly by `docs/03-modules/documents/MODULE_SPEC.md` `OD-DOC-012`/`SIR-DOC-012`) — remain open per-module boundary items (`SIR-COMPLIANCE-002`/`003`), not resolved by `ADR-016`, but not independently blocking either.

| Candidate | Summary | Evidence | Required action |
|---|---|---|---|
| `OD-COMPLIANCE-001` | **RESOLVED.** Whether `backend-compliance`/`SPEC-COMPLIANCE-001` becomes the authoritative writer of `state-audit-log` (`AuditLog`), whether it becomes a read-only governance consumer of a still-shared-write table, or whether a different module (e.g. `backend-auth`) is assigned instead. `ADR-016` settles it: `backend-auth` is authoritative writer; `backend-compliance` is read-only consumer. | `.claude/knowledge/STATE_OWNERSHIP_INDEX.yaml:27`; `backend/prisma/schema.prisma:507-533`; `SIR-GLOB-006`; `SYNC-005`; `docs/14-governance/architecture-decisions/ADR-016-audit-log-ownership-auth-writer-compliance-reader.md` | None outstanding — closed by `ADR-016`. `state-user`'s dual-writer half of `SYNC-005`/`SIR-GLOB-006` remains OPEN, unaffected. |
